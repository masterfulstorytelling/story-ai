# Corporate Storytelling Evaluation Tool - Report Specification

## Report Philosophy

This is not a typical "AI analysis" report. This is a **business crisis diagnosis** that:
- Names the specific problem the company is facing
- Quantifies the problem with objective-feeling scores
- Provides devastating evidence (real citations)
- Proves the problem is complex (thorough analysis)
- Offers a clear path to expert help (Feedforward CTA)

**Tone:** Plainly honest testimony, not theatrical criticism. "I don't know what you do" is a valid assessment when backed by evidence.

## Scoring Rubrics (For Agents)

### Clarity Score (per audience, per question)

**What They Do (0-100):**
- 90-100: Crystal clear what product/service is
- 70-89: Generally clear with minor ambiguities
- 50-69: Somewhat unclear, requires inference
- 30-49: Very unclear, significant gaps
- 0-29: Cannot determine what they do

**How They're Different (0-100):**
- 90-100: Specific, measurable differentiation stated
- 70-89: Some differentiation but could be stronger
- 50-69: Vague differentiation ("innovative", "best")
- 30-49: Generic claims anyone could make
- 0-29: No differentiation visible

**Who Uses Them (0-100):**
- 90-100: Specific target customers named
- 70-89: General category clear (enterprises, SMBs)
- 50-69: Some indication but vague
- 30-49: Unclear who would use this
- 0-29: No indication of target customer

### Technical Appropriateness (0-100)

For each audience, measure gap between content level and audience level:
- 90-100: Perfect match
- 70-89: Slight mismatch but workable
- 50-69: Noticeable mismatch
- 30-49: Significant mismatch
- 0-29: Completely inappropriate level

### Jargon Density (0-100)
- 0-20: Minimal jargon, broadly understandable
- 21-40: Some jargon but explained
- 41-60: Moderate jargon, some explanation
- 61-80: High jargon, little explanation
- 81-100: Extreme jargon, incomprehensible to non-experts

### Vagueness (0-100)
- 0-20: Highly specific, concrete claims
- 21-40: Mostly specific with some generalizations
- 41-60: Mix of specific and vague
- 61-80: Mostly vague, few specifics
- 81-100: Extremely vague, could describe anything

### Memorability (0-100)
Count memorable phrases / total phrases analyzed:
- 80-100: 60%+ memorable (rare excellence)
- 50-79: 30-60% memorable
- 20-49: 10-30% memorable
- 0-19: <10% memorable

Memorable = specific, concrete, surprising, rhythmic, or emotionally resonant

### Importance Conveyed (0-100, per audience)
- 90-100: Clear problem, clear value, clear stakes
- 70-89: Problem or value clear, not both
- 50-69: Vague problem/value statements
- 30-49: Little indication of importance
- 0-29: No indication why audience should care

## Business Problem Detection Patterns

### Pattern 1: Champion Problem
**Signals:**
- High technical audience clarity (75+)
- Low business audience clarity (<40)
- High technical mismatch (70+)
- High jargon (70+)

**Business Impact:**
- Lost deals at executive review
- Long sales cycles
- High win rate â†’ low conversion

### Pattern 2: Talent Problem
**Signals:**
- Low memorability (<30)
- Low employee clarity (<50)
- Low importance to employees (<40)
- High vagueness (70+)

**Business Impact:**
- Low offer acceptance
- High comp premiums
- Low referral rates

### Pattern 3: Morale Problem
**Signals:**
- Low employee clarity (<50)
- Low importance to employees (<30)
- High vagueness (65+)
- Low memorability (<30)

**Business Impact:**
- Low engagement scores
- High turnover
- Can't articulate mission

### Pattern 4: General Messaging Failure
**Signals:**
- Low clarity across ALL audiences (<50)
- High vagueness (70+)
- High jargon (70+)
- Low differentiation (<30)

**Business Impact:**
- Multiple problems compounding
- Needs comprehensive overhaul
